DC MOTOR WINDMILL

OVERVIEW

The DC Motor Windmill project involves designing and constructing a small-scale wind turbine that converts wind energy into electrical energy using a DC motor as the generator. This project demonstrates basic principles of renewable energy generation, mechanical-to-electrical energy conversion, and sustainable engineering practices.

OPERATION

When wind blows, it turns the blades of the windmill. The rotation of the blades spins the DC motor, which converts the mechanical energy into electrical energy. The generated electricity is then transmitted through wires to the connected load, demonstrating the windmill's capability to produce power.

TOUCH SENSOR BUZZER.

Project Overview

The Touch Buzzer Sensor project is designed to demonstrate a simple yet effective way to interact with electronic systems through tactile feedback. This project integrates a touch sensor and a buzzer to create a system that responds to physical touch with an audible signal. It can serve as an educational tool for learning basic electronics and programming concepts or as a prototype for more complex applications like touch-activated alarms or interactive exhibits.

OPERATION

1.Power on the system. 2.Touch the sensor. 3.When the sensor is touched, the buzzer will emit a sound. 4.The system will reset and be ready for the next touch after a short delay.
